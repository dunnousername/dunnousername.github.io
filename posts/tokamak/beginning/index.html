tokamak: the beginning

I’ve had many computers.

Some are still here. They’re powered on, or they’re in a closet waiting to be looked at again. Others are lost, many are forgotten, and too many are left behind.

There are several computers that I keep around, either out of utility or for their history.

Two in particularly come to mind - tokamak, and toshiba.

This is the story of tokamak.

The beginning

tokamak was the first computer and the last computer I’ve ever built, in a way, even though I’ve built several. tokamak actually stems from the first computer I’ve ever built, which didn’t have a name. I remember having an Ubuntu install with the hostname FusionReactor, which is where the name tokamak comes from, but I’m not entirely sure whether FusionReactor was the original system or another computer.

I built my first computer, which we’ll assume was in fact FusionReactor, when I was 13 or so. It had a bunch of poor design choices, but I eventually completed the entire thing after some help from a local computer shop called CDW. At the time it was one of the most important experiences I ever had.

And for that reason it held a very special place in my heart.

In terms of technical specifications, it was pretty awful. It had a Celeron G1620 for a CPU, which I cannot recommend anyone buy. Don’t buy a Celeron when you could get a Pentium for an extra $5-10.

Anyway, I grew out of this computer, later using an FX-6300 system, and then a Ryzen 5 2600 system. FusionReactor was collecting dust. At the same time, I was looking at building my first RAID array. These paths ended up colliding.

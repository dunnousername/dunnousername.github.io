tokamak: from the ashes

tokamak wasn’t a new build. It comes from an older system, one with sentimental value, and it inherits this.

From the ashes rise a new server

Pretty much only the motherboard was the same. It was a socket LGA 1155, and I upgraded from a Celeron G1620 to a Xeon E3-1220 V2, which isn’t even supported on this entry-level motherboard, but somehow worked after adding a discrete GPU in the x1 slot.

I installed Artix Linux on it at the suggestion of someone I knew.

It has a large 6TB btrfs partition spread across two 6TB HGST drives in software RAID 1. I used an LSI 9240-8i flashed as an old host-bus adapter to connect the drives. The partition was initially fully encrypted, but that was a horrible idea and I don’t recommend it.

tokamak is likely serving this site right now. We’ll see how long I can take this system with me.

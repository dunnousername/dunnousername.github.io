Technical

This site is yet another iteration of my personal blog.

It’s currently based on the base16 hugo theme. There will likely be more details on the hardware and software in the future, but for now, this is a simple test page only.

The shortened history of page0, before I forget

My original blog used Jekyll on GitHub pages.

I didn’t like the themes it had and I found it hard to work with in some weird use cases.

Also it was written on Ruby, and I didn’t want the work of installing another programming language I don’t know how to use on my server if I were to host it in the future.

Fast forward a few months, and tokamak (blog post in the future?) is my new server. Then I get bored and write a few hundred lines of code, write my own CMS using Markdown and Jinja2 on top of Flask and Gunicorn, and go on a wild containerization spree.

Then I start getting back into running Archive Team Warriors (and you should too). I had a competition with one of my friends to try and get higher on the leaderboards. The leaderboards have a “stream view” that shows items as they are uploaded to the servers. The idea is that this encourages people to run more instances because their name shows up more often.

Anyway, I found a nickname like wwwvegardnet on the boards, and I decided to go that site. They had a really nice site, and after reading through some of the articles, I found that they used Hugo as a CMS.

At this point I had never heard of Hugo, but I looked at it and decided to replace dynsrv’s (blog post soon?) /pages/render endpoint with Hugo.

And here we are.
